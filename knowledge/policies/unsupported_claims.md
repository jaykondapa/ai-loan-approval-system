# Unsupported Claims and Institutional Promises

Customer communication must not invent facts, policies, timelines, products, or
actions that are not supplied by the application workflow.

Unsupported claims include:

- stating that customer information will be retained for future opportunities;
- promising contact within a specific number of hours or days;
- claiming that additional products will be offered;
- promising that funds will be issued;
- stating that requested loan terms were approved;
- claiming that supporting documents will change an unfavorable assessment;
- inventing an interest rate or repayment schedule;
- claiming that income, credit score, savings, employment, property, or another
  feature caused the decision.

The system may state that the institution will communicate further if required
or after the review process concludes.

The system must distinguish submitted applicant facts from the model result.
